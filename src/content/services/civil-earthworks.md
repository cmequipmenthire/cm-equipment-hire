Civil earthworks covers a broad range of ground work — from building farm roads and access tracks through to commercial site preparation and bulk earthmoving on large-scale civil projects. Whatever the scope, the fundamentals are the same: move the right amount of dirt to the right place, compact it properly, and leave a finished result that holds up.

## Road Construction and Maintenance

Whether it's a new farm road connecting paddocks to the shearing shed, or a properly engineered access road for a rural subdivision, we build roads that handle the traffic they'll see. Our Cat 740 dump truck carts material while the Cat 336E excavator shapes the road bed, cuts drains, and places gravel. We also maintain and repair existing roads — regrading, drainage clearing, and patching washouts.

## Site Preparation

Getting a site ready for construction means stripping topsoil, cutting to level, removing unsuitable material, and compacting the pad. We handle site preparation for sheds, houses, commercial buildings, and infrastructure projects. The excavator and dump truck combination lets us strip, cart, and shape efficiently without needing a convoy of different machines on site.

## Bulk Earthmoving

Some jobs are simply about moving large volumes of material. Whether it's fill for a building pad, spoil from an excavation, or gravel for road construction, our equipment is sized for bulk work. The Cat 740's 38-tonne payload means fewer loads and faster cycle times, keeping projects on schedule and on budget.

## Trenching and Services

The Cat 336E excavator digs clean, accurate trenches for water mains, sewer lines, electrical conduit, and drainage pipes. We work to the required depths and grades, and can backfill and compact once services are installed. For rural properties, this often means running water lines from dams or bores to troughs and tanks across the property.

## Farm Roads and Access Tracks

Good farm roads save time, reduce vehicle wear, and make property management easier. We build access tracks to paddocks, dams, sheds, and remote parts of your property — properly graded with drainage so they last through winter without turning into mud tracks.
