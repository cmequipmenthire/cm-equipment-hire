Land clearing on Wheatbelt properties takes many forms — from annual firebreak maintenance to full paddock reclamation where scrub and regrowth have taken over. Whatever the scope, the goal is the same: leave the ground clean, level, and ready for whatever comes next, whether that's fencing, seeding, or further earthworks.

## Fence Line Clearing

Overgrown fence lines are a constant maintenance headache. Trees and scrub push into fences, drop limbs on wires, and make inspection and repair difficult. Our Cat 336E excavator clears fence lines efficiently, removing trees and stumps from the fence corridor and leaving a clean strip that's easy to maintain.

## Firebreaks

Firebreaks are a legal requirement and a practical necessity across most of WA. We construct new firebreaks and maintain existing ones, clearing vegetation to the width and standard required by your local shire. Getting firebreaks done before the November deadline means one less thing to worry about heading into summer.

## Paddock Reclamation

Paddocks that have been left for a few years can go back to scrub surprisingly fast in the Wheatbelt. Reclaiming that country means removing regrowth, pulling stumps, raking roots, and getting the surface back to a state where you can run machinery over it safely. We clear thoroughly so you don't find hidden stumps with your seeder bar at the worst possible time.

## Stump Removal and Rock Work

Stumps and rocks left behind after clearing are a constant risk to farming equipment. We remove stumps and rake roots from the soil, and can also pick and pile rocks to clear paddocks for cropping. The excavator's reach and breakout force make short work of even large stumps and buried boulders.

**Note:** Clearing native vegetation in WA may require a permit from the Department of Water and Environmental Regulation (DWER) or the Department of Biodiversity, Conservation and Attractions (DBCA). Exemptions exist for certain activities including firebreak maintenance and regrowth clearing on previously cultivated land, but it's your responsibility to confirm compliance before work begins.
